Specialists to Consult

Essential

SpecialistWhy
Clinical GeneticistUnderstand the specific COL11A1 mutation, genetic counseling, coordinate care
Pediatric Orthopedic Spine SpecialistEvaluate spondyloepiphyseal dysplasia, scoliosis, vertebral abnormalities
Pediatric RheumatologistManage early-onset osteoarthritis and chronic joint/spine pain
Pediatric Pain Medicine SpecialistComprehensive chronic pain management program

Important Supportive Specialists

SpecialistWhy
Physical Therapist (experienced with connective tissue disorders)Core strengthening, spine stabilization, activity guidance
Pediatric Ophthalmologist / Vitreoretinal SpecialistAnnual monitoring for myopia, cataracts, retinal detachment; evaluate prophylactic cryoretinopexy
AudiologistAnnual hearing evaluations for progressive sensorineural loss; balance assessment
Pediatric Physiatrist (Physical Medicine & Rehabilitation)Overall musculoskeletal management and pain rehab
Pediatric CardiologistBaseline echocardiogram to screen for hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, mitral valve prolapse
Psychologist (specializing in chronic pain)CBT for pain management, coping skills, school adjustment

Where to Find Specialized Care

  • Skeletal Dysplasia Programs: Nemours Children's Health, Boston Children's Hospital, Children's Hospital Colorado, Seattle Children's, Shriners Children's, Cincinnati Children's
  • NORD Rare Disease Centers of Excellence: rarediseases.org
  • NIH Undiagnosed Diseases Network: 15 clinical sites nationwide for complex rare disease cases
  • Academic medical centers with connective tissue disorder clinics
  • The Paley Institute (specialized skeletal dysplasia orthopedic management)
