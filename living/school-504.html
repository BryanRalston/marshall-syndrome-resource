School 504 Plan Template

A 504 Plan is a legally binding document under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act that ensures your child receives accommodations to access education on equal footing with peers. The following accommodations are specifically relevant to Marshall syndrome with spinal pain. You can use this as a starting template when meeting with the school's 504 team.

Discuss with your doctor: Ask the medical team for a letter supporting these accommodations. A letter on medical letterhead that briefly explains the diagnosis and its functional impact carries significant weight in 504 meetings.

Classroom Accommodations

  • Sit-stand desk or standing desk option — the child should be able to alternate between sitting and standing throughout the day. Sustained sitting is as harmful as sustained standing; the ability to change position is the key accommodation.
  • Permission to change positions during class — standing, stretching, shifting position without needing to ask or draw attention. This should be normalized, not treated as a disruption.
  • 30-minute movement break schedule — brief (2–5 minute) movement breaks every 30 minutes during seated instruction. This can be as simple as standing, walking to the water fountain, or doing gentle stretches at the back of the room.
  • Ergonomic seating — supportive chair with lumbar support, or permission to use a seat cushion and/or footrest
  • Preferential seating — near the door (for movement breaks) and with clear sightlines to visual aids (for vision accommodations if needed)

Materials and Supplies

  • Duplicate textbooks — one set at school, one at home. This eliminates the need to carry heavy books back and forth. Backpack weight should not exceed 10% of body weight (or use a rolling bag).
  • Digital textbooks when available — eliminates weight entirely
  • Rolling backpack permitted even if not standard at the school
  • Laptop or tablet for notetaking — reduces the physical strain of sustained handwriting (if writing is painful due to hand joint involvement)

Physical Education

  • Modified PE, not exemption — the child should participate in PE with specific modifications, not be excused entirely. Exercise is therapeutic (see Exercise as Medicine).
  • Safe activities: Swimming, cycling/stationary bike, walking, modified yoga, Pilates-based exercises, lightweight resistance bands, balance exercises
  • Activities to avoid: Running (sustained or sprinting), contact sports, trampolining, gymnastics, heavy lifting, dodgeball or activities with risk of impact to head/face
  • PE should not be first period (discs are maximally pressurized in the morning)
  • The PE teacher should be provided with the specific safe/avoid activity lists

Building Access

  • Elevator access if the school is multi-story
  • Extra time between classes to avoid rushing on stairs or through crowded hallways
  • Locker placement in a central location to minimize walking with heavy materials

Health and Pain Management

  • Nurse access for pain management — permission to visit the school nurse for medication, ice/heat, or a quiet rest period during pain flares without requiring teacher approval each time
  • Permission to carry water bottle at all times (hydration supports disc health)
  • Excused absences for medical appointments and pain flare days without academic penalty
  • Modified homework load during flare periods — extended deadlines, not reduced expectations
  • Access to a quiet space during pain episodes

Emergency Information

  • School nurse should have on file: diagnosis details, emergency contact protocol, and the retinal detachment emergency description (sudden vision changes = call 911)
  • List of current medications and when they can be administered
  • Contact information for the child's primary specialist
