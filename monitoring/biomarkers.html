Monitoring research synthesis. Biomarkers and assessment tools described here are based on published research. Their specific application to tracking Marshall syndrome progression is our interpretive framework. Work with your medical team to determine appropriate monitoring.

This section provides a printable checklist of blood biomarkers and assessments specifically relevant to COL11A1 disc disease. Bring this to your next appointment.

Collection note: For consistency, blood draws should be standardized at 10–11 AM when possible. Many biomarkers have diurnal variation, and standardizing collection time allows meaningful comparison between tests over time.

Daily Resilience Tracking Established

  • HRV (Heart Rate Variability) via wearable — a daily, non-invasive measure of autonomic nervous system resilience. Higher HRV generally correlates with better stress adaptation and pain tolerance. Wearable devices (Apple Watch, Whoop, Oura) can track this automatically. Useful for identifying patterns: does HRV drop before pain flares? Does it improve with exercise?

Pain Phenotyping Assessment Established

Ask your doctor about: The DFNS QST protocol (German Research Network on Neuropathic Pain Quantitative Sensory Testing). This is a standardized battery of 13 sensory parameters that is feasible from age 9+. It objectively distinguishes between:
  • Nociceptive pain (from tissue damage — responds to NSAIDs, PT)
  • Neuropathic pain (from nerve damage — responds to gabapentin, SNRIs)
  • Nociplastic pain (from central sensitization — responds to CBT, exercise, duloxetine)

Each type requires a fundamentally different treatment approach. Without phenotyping, treatment is guesswork.
