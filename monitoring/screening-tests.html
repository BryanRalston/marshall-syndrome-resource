Expanded Panel (from Latest Research)

TestWhat It ChecksWhy It Matters
VDR genotyping (FokI, TaqI, BsmI, ApaI)Vitamin D receptor gene variantsCommercially available. Unfavorable VDR variants modify how effectively the body uses vitamin D, which affects collagen synthesis and disc health. Results guide vitamin D dosing — some genotypes need higher targets.
Omega-3 IndexOmega-3 percentage in red blood cell membranesSimple dried blood spot test (home kits available). Target 8–12%. Most Americans are 4–5%. Confirms whether omega-3 supplementation is actually reaching therapeutic levels. Guides EPA dosing adjustments.
Orthostatic vitals for dysautonomiaHeart rate and blood pressure response to standingFree bedside test. Protocol: 5 min supine, then stand for 10 min. A heart rate increase of 40+ bpm (pediatric threshold) = POTS (Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome). Dysautonomia is common in connective tissue disorders and causes fatigue, dizziness, and pain amplification. Easy to miss if not tested for.
Vestibular assessment (VEMP, VNG)Inner ear balance organ functionMouse models of COL11A1 mutations show reduced vestibular stereocilia (the tiny hair cells in the balance organs). Vestibular impairment compounds spinal instability by degrading the brain's ability to coordinate postural muscles. Tests: VEMP (vestibular evoked myogenic potential) and VNG (videonystagmography).
Tethered cord screeningSpinal cord mobility at base of spineAsk about: bladder symptoms (urgency, accidents, incomplete emptying), toe-walking, skin stigmata over lower spine (dimple, hair tuft, discoloration). If any present, spinal MRI including conus level is indicated. Tethered cord is treatable and can mimic or worsen spinal pain.
PCS-C (Pain Catastrophizing Scale for Children)Pain-related thinking patternsBrief validated questionnaire. Score of 26+ is clinically elevated and predicts worse pain outcomes, greater disability, and poorer treatment response. If elevated, psychological intervention (CBT) should be prioritized alongside physical treatments. This is not about whether the pain is "real" — it is about how the brain processes it.
Bone density with BMAD correctionTrue bone mineral densityStandard DEXA is misleading with platyspondyly. Flattened vertebrae produce falsely normal or elevated density readings because DEXA measures area (2D), not volume (3D). Request BMAD (bone mineral apparent density) correction, which adjusts for vertebral body size. Without this correction, true osteopenia may be missed.
Tip: Not all of these tests need to be ordered at once. Prioritize based on the child's symptoms: if there is burning/tingling pain, prioritize the skin punch biopsy. If morning stiffness is prominent, prioritize CRP/ESR and tryptase. If pain worsens despite rest, prioritize MMP3 levels. The orthostatic vitals test is free and takes 15 minutes — there is no reason not to do it at the next visit.
