Telehealth for Rare Disease Specialist Access (R405)

Telehealth access expanded significantly in 2025–2026. Federal and state policies enacted during the pandemic have been made permanent or extended, dramatically improving specialist access for rare disease families who previously had to travel hundreds of miles for expert consultations.

For Marshall syndrome families, telehealth is particularly valuable because the syndrome requires coordination across multiple subspecialties (genetics, ophthalmology, audiology, orthopedics, pain medicine, PT) — specialists who rarely exist in the same geographic location.

Practical advice for telehealth visits:
  • Request a pre-visit summary upload — send recent imaging, audiograms, and lab results to the specialist’s portal before the appointment so the video time focuses on interpretation and planning, not data review
  • Keep a Marshall syndrome summary letter (diagnosis, mutation, current specialists, current medications, key concerns) that can be sent to any new telehealth provider in advance. This eliminates the need to re-explain the diagnosis at every new visit
  • Ask whether the specialist does virtual multidisciplinary team conferences — some academic centers offer coordinated virtual visits where multiple subspecialists participate in a single session, reducing the appointment burden on the family

Genetic Counseling

If not already done, the family should pursue formal genetic counseling to:

  • Understand the inheritance pattern (autosomal dominant — 50% chance of passing to each child)
  • Interpret their specific mutation and its known associations
  • Discuss family planning implications
  • Connect to appropriate registries and research
