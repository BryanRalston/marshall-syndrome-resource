Questions for Your Medical Team

About Imaging and Diagnosis

  1. "Has our child had a full-spine MRI to look for spondyloepiphyseal dysplasia, endplate irregularities, Schmorl's nodes, disc degeneration, or platyspondyly? These are documented in COL11A1-related conditions, even in children as young as 9."
  2. "Should we get standing X-rays of the full spine to evaluate for scoliosis and kyphosis?"
  3. "Is there evidence of premature disc degeneration or narrowing of disc spaces? COL11A1 is the gene most directly linked to intervertebral disc integrity in the human genome."
  4. "Has spinal stenosis been evaluated? This is a known risk in skeletal dysplasias."
  5. "What is the specific COL11A1 mutation and which exon is affected?"
  6. "Have cervical flexion-extension films been obtained to screen for atlantoaxial instability? This is recommended in COL11A1-related conditions."

About the Pain

  1. "Are you familiar with the spinal literature on Stickler/Marshall syndrome? Studies show 74% of patients have vertebral endplate abnormalities and 64% have Schmorl's nodes. This may not be a diagnostic mystery — it may be an expected manifestation of the underlying collagen disorder."
  2. "Could the pain be from early-onset osteoarthritis of the facet joints or vertebral endplates? Osteoarthritis prevalence in these patients is approximately 15% before age 20."
  3. "Could weakened disc structure from defective type XI collagen be allowing disc material to herniate into vertebral bodies (Schmorl's nodes) or bulge?"
  4. "Is there a neuromuscular component? A 2024 study found that COL11A1 mutations can cause axial weakness and muscle involvement."
  5. "Could there be a small fiber neuropathy component to the pain? Type XI collagen is present in nerve sheaths. A skin punch biopsy could test small fiber nerve density and potentially change the pain management approach."

About Monitoring During Growth

  1. "How are the vertebral growth plates being monitored? COL11A1 protein localizes to the vertebral growth plate region, and the growth spurt places maximal stress on these structures."
  2. "Should scoliosis monitoring increase to every 6 months during the growth spurt? Published guidelines recommend this for skeletal dysplasias."
  3. "What is the Risser stage and are we in the window where bracing would be effective if scoliosis progresses?"

About Emerging Diagnostics and Therapies

  1. "Can we check MMP3 blood levels? MMP3 is the enzyme shown to drive spinal cartilage degradation in COL11A1 mutations (2023 eLife study). Elevated levels would confirm this pathway is active and provide a biomarker for monitoring."
  2. "Is there a role for low-dose doxycycline as an MMP inhibitor to potentially slow disc degeneration? This is off-label but has precedent in other MMP-driven conditions."
  3. "Are you aware of the OrphanAnesthesia guidelines for Stickler/Marshall syndrome? We want to ensure any future anesthesia providers have disease-specific guidance."
  4. "Should we get a baseline echocardiogram to screen for hypertrophic cardiomyopathy or mitral valve prolapse, both reported in COL11A1 conditions?"

About Treatment and Long-Term Planning

  1. "Can we get a referral to a pediatric pain medicine program?"
  2. "Should we start physical therapy focused on core stabilization and spine support, with a therapist experienced in connective tissue disorders?"
  3. "Are NSAIDs safe for regular use, and should we add adjunctive medications if they're insufficient?"
  4. "What is the long-term monitoring plan for spinal progression? These abnormalities worsen with age."
  5. "Can you refer us to a skeletal dysplasia program or connective tissue disorder center for multidisciplinary evaluation?"
  6. "Are there any emerging therapy research programs we should be enrolled in or aware of? We're specifically interested in ASO splice correction trials, disc progenitor cell therapy (DiscGenics IDCT), and LIPUS for disc degeneration."

About Growth Plates and Underexplored Mechanisms

  1. "Have the vertebral growth plates been specifically evaluated on imaging? In a growing child with COL11A1, the growth plates — not just the endplates — may be the primary pain source. This may require T2 fat-sat or STIR MRI sequences."
  2. "Should we screen for sleep-disordered breathing? Midface hypoplasia and possible Pierre Robin features increase airway obstruction risk, and poor sleep amplifies pain perception."
  3. "Would sub-antimicrobial doxycycline be worth considering as an MMP inhibitor? There is RCT evidence for structural cartilage benefit in osteoarthritis (Brandt et al.)."
  4. "Is there a role for proprioceptive assessment? Collagen disorders affect the mechanoreceptors that control joint position sense."
  5. "Has the child's pain been assessed using a validated tool like the Adolescent Pediatric Pain Tool (APPT)? This has been validated in connective tissue disorders."
