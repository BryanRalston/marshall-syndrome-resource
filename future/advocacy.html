Rare Disease Advocacy

Families affected by Marshall syndrome face the challenge of advocating for a condition most doctors have never encountered.

FDA Plausible Mechanism Framework (February 2026)

New Opportunity: The FDA's updated Plausible Mechanism Framework allows approval based on mechanistic evidence rather than large-scale trials, which are impossible for ultra-rare conditions.
  • Drugs addressing the specific molecular mechanism could be approved without traditional RCTs
  • Existing FDA-approved drugs (like 4-PBA) could be repurposed with strong mechanistic rationale
  • N-of-1 trial data combined with mechanism may suffice for expanded access

N=1 Collaborative (Boston Children's Hospital)

  • Designs patient-specific therapies based on exact mutation
  • Successfully developed ASO therapies for single patients
  • Accepts referrals for patients with identified genetic mutations
  • Website: n1collaborative.org

NORD (National Organization for Rare Disorders)

  • Patient Assistance Programs for medication costs
  • Research grants and advocacy training
  • Website: rarediseases.org

Stickler Syndrome Support Group

  • Most relevant patient community (both are COL11A1 disorders)
  • Annual conference, family networking, specialist referral list
  • Website: stickler.org.uk

NIH Undiagnosed Diseases Program (UDP)

  • Free evaluation at NIH Clinical Center
  • Multidisciplinary team + genomic sequencing
  • Can identify additional genetic modifiers affecting severity
  • Referral by physician required
  • Website: NIH UDP

Building Your Advocacy Toolkit

  1. Create a one-page medical summary — mutation, key findings, treatments, emergency considerations
  2. Register in rare disease databases — GeneMatcher, NORD registry, ClinVar
  3. Connect with researchers — authors of relevant publications are often eager to hear from families
  4. Document everything — photos, imaging, labs, symptom diaries become future data points
  5. Consider genetic counseling
  6. Attend Rare Disease Day (last day of February)
