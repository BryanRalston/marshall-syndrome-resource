Epigenetic Defense Protocol

Epigenetics is the system that controls which genes are turned on or off without changing the DNA itself. Think of it as the volume knobs on your genes. In disc degeneration, certain destructive genes get turned up and protective genes get turned down. Four dietary compounds have disc-specific evidence for adjusting these knobs in the right direction.

1. Sulforaphane (Broccoli Sprouts)

Sulforaphane is the most evidence-backed compound in this group for disc health:

  • HDAC inhibitor — blocks enzymes that silence protective genes (turns the volume back up on disc repair programs)
  • NRF2 activator — activates the master antioxidant defense system
  • Anti-progerin — reduces production of the accelerated-aging protein progerin
  • TWO disc-specific studies demonstrate protective effects on intervertebral disc cells
  • Dose: 20–40 mg/day from broccoli sprout extract, or a handful of fresh broccoli sprouts daily

Broccoli sprouts have 10–100x more sulforaphane than mature broccoli. They can be grown at home in 3–5 days.

2. Curcumin (Turmeric)

  • Pan-HDAC inhibitor — more potent than valproic acid (a pharmaceutical HDAC inhibitor) at epigenetic modulation
  • Inhibits NF-kB p65 in disc cells — directly suppresses the master inflammatory switch in intervertebral disc tissue
  • Dose: 250–500 mg of a bioavailable form (must include piperine/BioPerine for absorption, or use a phospholipid formulation)
  • Standard turmeric powder in food is poorly absorbed — a bioavailable supplement is needed for therapeutic effect

3. Resveratrol

  • SIRT1 activator — SIRT1 is a longevity-associated enzyme that protects cells from senescence and promotes disc cell survival
  • Increases collagen II production in NP cells
  • Decreases MMP-13 and ADAMTS-5 — reduces the two key destructive enzymes in disc tissue
  • Dose: 50–100 mg/day
  • Dietary sources: grapes, berries, peanuts

4. Dietary Fiber for Butyrate

  • Gut bacteria ferment dietary fiber into butyrate, a short-chain fatty acid
  • Butyrate is a natural HDAC inhibitor — it reaches the bloodstream and modulates gene expression throughout the body, including in disc tissue
  • This connects to the Gut-Spine Connection — a healthy gut microbiome is an epigenetic defense system
  • Sources: whole grains, legumes, vegetables, fruits
Discuss with your doctor: These compounds have favorable safety profiles but should be reviewed alongside any medications. Curcumin can affect blood clotting and drug metabolism. Start one at a time to identify any sensitivities, and keep doses at the lower end of the ranges listed.
