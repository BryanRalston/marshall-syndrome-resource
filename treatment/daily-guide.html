Practical Daily Guide

Exercise Timing

Morning caution: Avoid heavy physical activity in the first 1–2 hours after waking. Intervertebral discs are maximally pressurized from overnight rehydration and are more susceptible to herniation during this window.
  • Land-based PT is best in mid-to-late morning or early afternoon
  • Swimming can be done anytime — buoyancy makes disc-pressure timing irrelevant
  • If school has gym class, request it NOT be first period
  • Mornings: Gentle stretching and proprioceptive work only

Sleep Optimization

  • Side-lying with pillow between knees is recommended (reduces spinal torsion)
  • Screen for sleep-disordered breathing: 24% Pierre Robin rate means elevated airway obstruction risk. If snoring, mouth breathing, or restless sleep are present, request a pediatric sleep study
  • Poor sleep amplifies pain through central sensitization AND impairs growth hormone secretion (critical for cartilage repair during childhood)
  • Consider a medium-firm mattress with good spinal support

Pain Tracking Journal

Consistent pain tracking serves two purposes: it helps identify which pain mechanisms are most active, and it creates documentation for doctors that is far more useful than a verbal report at an appointment.

  • Rate pain 0–10 morning and evening
  • Note quality: aching (mechanical), burning/tingling (neuropathic), sharp (acute), stiff (inflammatory)
  • Record what helped or made it worse
  • Track morning stiffness duration (>30 minutes suggests inflammatory component)
  • Note response to swimming — if water consistently helps, that supports the mechanical/neuromuscular mechanisms
  • Bring the journal to every medical appointment
