Why Repurposing Matters for Rare Diseases

Randomized controlled trials will never happen for Marshall syndrome. The population is too small, the phenotype too variable, and the funding too limited. But that does not mean evidence-based treatment is impossible.

  • Approved drugs with known pediatric safety profiles can be considered off-label. Off-label prescribing is legal, common, and in many cases the standard of care in pediatrics — up to 80% of pediatric prescriptions are off-label because most drugs are not studied in children
  • The key is mechanistic rationale: Does the drug target a pathway that is proven to be active in Marshall syndrome? If COL11A1 deficiency drives ER stress, and a drug is proven to reduce ER stress in other collagenopathies, the rationale is direct
  • N-of-1 trial framework: Formal methodology exists for generating single-patient evidence that is statistically valid and publishable.[40] This is not “trying things and hoping” — it is structured, measurable experimentation with defined endpoints

Carbamazepine (CBZ) — ER Stress Relief Supported

This may be the most compelling repurposing candidate. Carbamazepine has orphan drug designation from the EMA for a collagen misfolding disorder with the same cellular mechanism as Marshall syndrome.

Mechanism: Carbamazepine stimulates autophagy and reduces endoplasmic reticulum (ER) stress caused by misfolded collagen chains. It enhances the cell’s ability to clear defective protein before ER stress triggers apoptosis.

  • Evidence: In metaphyseal chondrodysplasia type Schmid (MCDS) — caused by collagen X mutations that produce misfolded protein — CBZ reduced ER stress markers, increased bone growth, and reduced skeletal dysplasia severity
  • Regulatory status: CBZ has received EMA orphan drug designation for MCDS, indicating regulatory recognition of its mechanism in collagen misfolding disorders
  • Why it applies to Marshall: Splice-site mutations in COL11A1 produce shortened alpha1(XI) chains missing 18 amino acids from the triple helix domain. This creates local helix instability → ER quality control detects misfolding → unfolded protein response (UPR) activation → chondrocyte apoptosis. CBZ helps clear misfolded protein via autophagy before the UPR reaches lethal thresholds
  • Pediatric safety: Decades of use in epilepsy with well-characterized dosing and monitoring protocols. Extensively studied in children
  • Key considerations: Requires blood level monitoring (therapeutic range well-established). Potential drug interactions via CYP3A4 induction. Risk of Stevens-Johnson syndrome (rare but serious — HLA-B*1502 screening recommended in at-risk populations)
  • Next step: Discuss with clinical genetics team. The MCDS orphan designation strengthens the case for off-label consideration

Chemical Chaperones — 4-PBA and TUDCA Supported

Chemical chaperones stabilize protein folding in the ER, reducing the burden of misfolded protein that triggers the UPR.[38] Two compounds have the most evidence:

4-PBA (Sodium Phenylbutyrate, brand: Buphenyl) Supported

  • FDA-approved for urea cycle disorders — established pediatric safety and dosing
  • 2025 evidence: Rescues dominant-negative collagen I mutations in zebrafish osteogenesis imperfecta by reducing ER cisternae size and favoring collagen secretion over intracellular retention[38]
  • Also rescues dominant-negative collagen III in vascular Ehlers-Danlos syndrome models
  • Dual action: Could reduce the ER stress component AND improve the ratio of functional vs. defective secreted collagen

TUDCA (Tauroursodeoxycholic Acid) Hypothesis

  • Reduces ER stress and has anti-apoptotic properties
  • Available as a supplement (OTC in some countries), also being studied as a pharmaceutical
  • More accessible than 4-PBA but less potent and less well-characterized for dosing
Marshall-specific rationale: COL11A1 splice-site mutations produce a dual mechanism — a dominant-negative secretion pathway (defective chains poison normal trimers) PLUS a partial ER stress component (misfolded chains trigger UPR). Chemical chaperones address both: they help misfolded protein fold correctly, reducing ER stress, and they favor secretion of the functional fraction.

Sub-Antimicrobial Doxycycline — MMP Inhibitor Supported

Mechanism: Doxycycline directly inhibits MMP-3 and MMP-13 — the exact matrix metalloproteinases overexpressed when COL11A1 is deficient.

  • Evidence: A randomized controlled trial (Brandt et al., n=431) showed significant slowing of joint space narrowing in osteoarthritis over 30 months of treatment[21]
  • Sub-antimicrobial dosing: 20mg BID in adults (scaled for pediatric weight) avoids antibiotic resistance concerns while retaining full MMP inhibition. The MMP-inhibitory mechanism is structurally independent of the antibiotic mechanism
  • Marshall-specific: MMP-3 overexpression is a direct consequence of failed COL11A1 suppression. The PAX1 → COL11A1 → MMP3 axis is the central pathogenic cascade in disc degeneration. Sub-antimicrobial doxycycline directly intervenes at the final effector step
Important caveats:
  • One mouse study found sub-antimicrobial doxycycline worsened bone microarchitecture — needs careful evaluation in a child with already-compromised bone
  • Not appropriate below age 8 due to permanent tooth discoloration risk — this child is 9, so the timing window has opened, but the risk-benefit must be weighed carefully
  • Safety profile in OA trials was good: only GI complaints slightly increased, no serious adverse events

Metformin — AMPK Activator Supported

Mechanism: Metformin activates AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK), which inhibits mTOR, reduces chondrocyte senescence, enhances autophagy, and directly protects cartilage extracellular matrix.[22]

  • Evidence: A 2025 Frontiers review explicitly discusses metformin as a disease-modifying osteoarthritis therapy, citing AMPK-mediated cartilage protection and anti-inflammatory effects[22]
  • Extraordinarily well-studied: Cheap, safe, and available now. Decades of pediatric use in type 2 diabetes with well-established dosing and monitoring
  • Anti-inflammatory via AMPK → reduced NF-κB: This reduces the inflammatory milieu that drives both cartilage degradation and central sensitization
  • Marshall-specific: mTOR activation drives chondrocyte hypertrophy and ECM breakdown. AMPK activation also feeds into NRF2 activation, connecting to the NRF2 convergence pathway — making metformin a node where multiple therapeutic mechanisms converge
  • Availability: Generic, inexpensive, available now with pediatric dosing well-established

Dimethyl Fumarate (DMF/Tecfidera) — NRF2 Activator Supported

DMF provides pharmaceutical-grade NRF2 activation — more potent and dose-controllable than dietary NRF2 activators like sulforaphane.[73] See the NRF2 Convergence page for the full mechanistic rationale.

  • Direct disc cell data: Reduces MMP-13 and IL-1β expression in nucleus pulposus cells — the exact cell type undergoing degeneration in Marshall syndrome
  • Pediatric safety: Established in the FOCUS trial (ages 10+) for multiple sclerosis. Known side effect profile including flushing and GI symptoms
  • Advantage over supplements: Pharmaceutical manufacturing ensures consistent dosing, bioavailability, and purity that dietary supplements cannot guarantee

Omega-3 Fatty Acids — Targeted MMP-13 Suppressor Supported

Omega-3 fatty acids are not just “generally anti-inflammatory” — EPA (eicosapentaenoic acid) specifically inhibits MMP-13 expression through the NF-κB pathway.[31]

  • Specificity: EPA is the active component for MMP suppression.[31] DHA has different (complementary) effects but is not the primary MMP-13 inhibitor
  • Dose-response: May have an inverted U-shape — moderate doses appear more effective than very high doses for MMP suppression
  • Safety: Excellent safety profile, no significant drug interactions, well-tolerated in children
  • Availability: OTC, but consider pharmaceutical-grade omega-3 (Lovaza or Vascepa) for consistent EPA content and dosing. Supplement-grade products vary widely in actual EPA content

AI-Driven Drug Repurposing: The Computational Frontier (R399) Supported

95% of rare diseases lack an FDA-approved treatment. For ultra-rare conditions like Marshall syndrome, the traditional drug development pipeline — target identification, lead optimization, Phase I–III trials — will never produce a dedicated therapy. AI-driven drug repurposing is emerging as the most promising shortcut.

Computational drug repurposing uses machine learning models trained on protein structures, gene expression profiles, drug-target interaction databases, and clinical outcome data to identify existing approved medications that may have therapeutic activity against new disease targets. For rare diseases, this approach has several critical advantages:

  • Foundation models for protein-drug interaction: Large-scale AI models (such as AlphaFold-derived drug interaction predictors) can now screen millions of approved drug-target combinations against the specific protein defects in COL11A1 disorders. These models identify candidates based on structural complementarity, pathway overlap, and expression-signature matching — analyses that would take decades using traditional methods
  • Success precedent — nitisinone in alkaptonuria: Nitisinone, originally a herbicide approved for hereditary tyrosinemia type 1, was identified through computational pathway analysis as a potential treatment for alkaptonuria (another ultra-rare genetic disorder). It is now in clinical use for alkaptonuria, demonstrating that AI-guided repurposing produces real clinical therapies, not just hypothetical candidates
  • Pathway-level screening rather than single-target: AI models can evaluate drugs based on their effects across entire pathways (ER stress, NF-kB, MMP cascade, NRF2 activation) rather than single molecular targets. This is particularly relevant for Marshall syndrome, where the pathology involves multiple converging pathways rather than a single druggable target
  • Accelerated timeline: Because repurposed drugs already have established safety profiles, pediatric dosing data, and manufacturing infrastructure, the time from computational identification to clinical use can be months rather than years. For Marshall syndrome families, a repurposed drug could potentially be prescribed off-label as soon as the mechanistic rationale is validated
What this means now. Families and clinicians should monitor rare disease drug repurposing databases and AI-generated candidate lists. Organizations like the National Center for Advancing Translational Sciences (NCATS) and the Rare Diseases Clinical Research Network (RDCRN) maintain repurposing screens. As AI-identified candidates for collagenopathy pathways emerge, the N-of-1 trial framework described below provides the methodology to test them in individual patients.

The N-of-1 Trial Framework Supported

For an ultra-rare disease like Marshall syndrome, the N-of-1 trial is not a compromise — it is the optimal study design. It provides the strongest possible evidence for an individual patient.

  • Design: ABAB crossover — baseline (A) → intervention (B) → washout (A) → intervention (B). Each patient serves as their own control, eliminating between-patient variability
  • 2024 Nature Communications provides formal statistical methods for N-of-1 trials, establishing them as rigorous science rather than anecdote[40]
  • New FDA guidance creates a regulatory pathway for individualized therapies, recognizing that traditional RCTs are impossible for many rare diseases
  • Bayesian methods: Require 30–2400% fewer participants than frequentist approaches to reach the same statistical confidence

Practical implementation:

  1. 3-month baseline with standardized measurements: pain VAS (visual analog scale), disc height on MRI, biomarkers (COMP, MMP-3 serum levels), functional scores (CHAQ or equivalent)
  2. Introduce intervention with continued measurement at defined intervals
  3. Washout period (duration depends on drug half-life) with continued measurement
  4. Reintroduce intervention with continued measurement
  5. Statistical analysis comparing A periods to B periods using Bayesian methods

This framework empowers the family to generate publishable, statistically valid evidence that can inform treatment for other Marshall syndrome patients.

Avacopan (Tavneos) — Complement C5a Receptor Antagonist Speculative

Mechanism: Avacopan is an oral C5a receptor (C5aR1) antagonist. It blocks the inflammatory signaling driven by the complement anaphylatoxin C5a without blocking MAC (membrane attack complex) formation — preserving terminal complement defense while shutting down the inflammatory amplifier.

  • FDA-approved 2021 for ANCA-associated vasculitis. Oral dosing, established safety profile
  • The complement gap: Of all 8 self-amplifying cascades in the Marshall model, Cascade 6 (Complement Sump) was the ONLY cascade with zero drug coverage from any existing candidate. Avacopan fills this gap
  • The complement-senescence bridge (2024): Angerbaur et al. (Frontiers in Immunology, 2024) proved that terminal complement complex deposition on chondrocytes directly causes premature senescence (p16, p21 upregulation, SASP induction). CD59-knockout mice developed more severe OA. Critically, NAC (antioxidant) had zero anti-senescent effect — it is the cytokines (IL-6, IL-1β) from TCC, not oxidative stress, that drive complement-induced senescence. This means complement inhibition slows both Cascade 6 AND Cascade 3 (senescence)
  • Marshall-specific: In the avascular disc, complement products accumulate because there is no vascular clearance. Collagen fragments from MMP degradation activate complement as DAMPs, creating a self-amplifying loop: fragments → complement → TCC → senescence → SASP → more MMPs → more fragments
  • See full analysis: Immune Checkpoint Biology in the Disc and The Complement Sump
Critical caveat: Avacopan has never been tested in disc degeneration, osteoarthritis, or any collagenopathy. The rationale is entirely based on the complement-senescence bridge finding and the known role of complement in disc pathology. It is the most speculative drug on this list. Pediatric safety data is limited (no studies under 18 for this drug specifically).
Complement-hearing paradox. While complement inhibition may protect discs and eyes, complement factor B appears to be protective in the cochlea. Factor B-knockout mice develop progressive hearing loss, spiral ganglion neuron damage, and stria vascularis abnormalities. Systemic complement inhibition could therefore harm hearing in Marshall syndrome patients who already have vulnerable tectorial membranes. If complement-targeting therapy is ever considered, intravitreal delivery (eye-only, as used in FDA-approved GA treatments) would avoid this risk. See the Multi-Organ Protection Strategy for details.

Resolvin D1 — Endogenous Pro-Resolution Mediator Supported

Mechanism: Resolvin D1 (RvD1) is an endogenous specialized pro-resolving mediator derived from EPA/DHA metabolism. It is not a drug but is enhanced by adequate omega-3 intake.

  • Triple action in the disc: (1) Prevents MerTK cleavage from macrophage surfaces, restoring efferocytosis (dead cell clearance); (2) promotes M1-to-M2 macrophage polarization; (3) directly inhibits NF-κB and MMP expression
  • The corpse problem: Ferroptosis and Cx43 death waves generate dead cells. Without efficient efferocytosis, these corpses release DAMPs that activate complement and recruit more immune cells. RvD1 breaks this loop
  • Practical implication: This strengthens the rationale for omega-3 (EPA) supplementation — adequate EPA substrate is needed for the body to produce RvD1. Target Omega-3 Index ≥8%
